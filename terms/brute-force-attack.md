
# Brute Force Attack

## Definition

A brute force attack is a method attackers use to gain unauthorised access by repeatedly trying different username and password combinations until the correct credentials are found.

## Why It Matters

Weak passwords are vulnerable to brute force attacks. Strong password policies and Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) significantly reduce this risk.

## Example

An attacker attempts thousands of password combinations against an employee's Microsoft 365 account before triggering an account lockout.
