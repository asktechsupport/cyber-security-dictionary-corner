# Indicator of Compromise (IOC)

## Definition

An Indicator of Compromise (IOC) is evidence that suggests a system has been compromised. Common IOCs include malicious IP addresses, domains, URLs, file hashes, registry keys, and process names.

## Why It Matters

Analysts search for IOCs across their environment to identify infected devices and determine the scope of an incident.

## Example

Threat intelligence identifies a malicious SHA-256 file hash associated with ransomware, allowing analysts to search all endpoints for the file.
